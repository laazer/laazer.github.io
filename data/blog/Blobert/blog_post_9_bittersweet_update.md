# Blobert Devlog #9: Bittersweet Update

Blobert has grown far beyond the portfolio project it started as. Over the past year it has evolved into a full game alongside a growing collection of tools, pipelines, and technology that I’m actively investing in.

Because of that, I’ve made the decision to transition the project from open source to closed source.

This wasn’t an easy decision. I strongly believe in sharing knowledge, and many of the ideas behind Blobert have been shaped by the incredible open-source community. As the project has matured, however, it has become clear that keeping the entire codebase public no longer aligns with my long-term goals for the game.

While the source code will no longer be publicly available, I don’t want the development process to disappear behind closed doors.

I’ll continue documenting the journey through development blogs, technical write-ups, architecture discussions, design decisions, postmortems, and behind-the-scenes looks at the systems powering Blobert. Whenever possible, I’ll also continue releasing standalone tools, experiments, and reusable components as open source.

If you’ve been following Blobert from the beginning, thank you. Your feedback, ideas, and encouragement have helped shape the project into what it is today. I hope you’ll continue following along as the game moves toward release.

The code may be private now, but the journey is still open.
